1. Abstract

Conveying the linguistic content and maintaining the source speaker's speaking style is essential in the voice conversion (VC) task. However, in the low-resource situation, which means only very limited utterances from the target speaker are accessible, existing VC methods are hard to meet this requirement and meanwhile convert the source speech to that with the timbre of the target speaker. In this work, a novel VC model with multi-factor constraints, referred to as MFC-StyleVC, is proposed for the low-resource VC task. In MFC-StyleVC, multi-factor constraints are proposed to explicitly constrain the learning of model on specific aspects, including speaker similarity, speech content, and speaking style. Meanwhile, a real/fake discriminator is adopted as a speech quality constraint through adversarial training to further prevent quality degradation in adaptation. Besides, a simulation training mode is introduced to simulate the inference process during the training to alleviate the mismatch between training and inference. Extensive experiments performed on the highly expressive speech demonstrate that MFC-StyleVC achieves a good performance on the VC task with very limited recordings. Furthermore, ablation analysis indicates the indispensable effectiveness of each constraint and the good design of MFC-StyleVC.

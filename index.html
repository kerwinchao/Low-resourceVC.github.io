1. Abstract

In voice conversion (VC) task, besides conveying linguistic content of source speech to converted speech, delivering speaking style is also essential in many scenarios with high expressive source speech, such as dubbing, live broadcasting, etc. In a low-resource situation, i.e., only a few utterances of the target speaker, current VC systems have difficulty achieving the goal of delivering speaking style while learning the timbre of a low-resource speaker. In this work, we propose a novel VC model with multi-factor constraints, referred to as MFC-StyleVC, for the low-resource VC task.Multi-factor constraints are proposed to design explicit constraints for different speech factors to help the model search for optimal parameters during adaptation. Specifically, The speaker timbre constraint for high-resource and low-resource speakers are generated by pre-trained speaker classifier and speaker indicator, respectively. The speaker classifier identifies the speaker's identity. The contrastive learning based speaker indicator determines the consistency of speaker timbre between converted speech and target speaker speech during adaptation. For speaking style and linguistic content, speech emotion recognition model and content model are introduced to calculate the loss of style and content. Meanwhile, a real/fake discriminator is adopted as a speech quality constraint through adversarial training to further prevent quality degradation in adaptation. With the help of these constraints, we simulate the inference process during the training to alleviate the mismatch between training and inference. Extensive experiments performed on the highly expressive speech testset demonstrate that MFC-StyleVC achieves a good performance of speaking style delivering and accurately clone the low-resource speaker with a few recordings. Furthermore, ablation analysis indicates the indispensable effectiveness of each constraint.
